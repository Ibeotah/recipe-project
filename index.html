Jollof Beans and Plantain

Jollof beans and plantain is a popular West African dish that combines two distinct but complementary foods. It's a staple in many households, particularly in Nigeria. The dish typically consists of beans that are cooked in a savory, spiced tomato and pepper stew, similar to the base of jollof rice. The addition of fried sweet plantain elevates the dish, providing a delightful contrast of textures and a subtle sweetness that balances the savory, spicy stew. It is a hearty and satisfying one-pot meal often enjoyed for lunch or dinner.

Recipe Information

  • Prep time: 20 minutes
  • Cooking time: 1 hour 30 minutes
  • Servings: 4-6
  • Difficulty: Intermediate

Ingredients

  • 2 cups black-eyed beans
  • 1 ripe plantain
  • 1 large onion, chopped
  • 4 ripe tomatoes
  • 2 red bell peppers (tatashe)
  • 1 scotch bonnet pepper (ata rodo), adjust to taste
  • 1/2 cup palm oil
  • 2 stock cubes
  • Salt to taste
  • Smoked fish or meat (optional)
  • Water for cooking

Instructions

  1. Soak and boil the beans: Rinse the beans and soak them in water for at least 30 minutes. Drain and place them in a pot with fresh water. Boil until the beans are soft but not mushy. Drain and set aside.
  2. Prepare the pepper mix: Blend the tomatoes, red bell peppers, scotch bonnet pepper, and half of the onion until smooth.
  3. Sauté the aromatics: Heat the palm oil in a separate pot. Add the remaining chopped onion and sauté until translucent.
  4. Cook the stew base: Pour the blended pepper mix into the pot with the palm oil. Add the stock cubes and a pinch of salt. Cook on medium heat, stirring occasionally, until the stew has thickened and the oil has settled on top. This should take about 15-20 minutes.
  5. Combine the beans and stew: Add the cooked beans to the stew. If using, add smoked fish or meat at this point. Stir well to combine, add a little water if needed, and let it simmer for 10-15 minutes, allowing the flavors to meld.
  6. Fry the plantain: While the beans are simmering, slice the ripe plantain and fry in a separate pan with vegetable oil until golden brown.
  7. Serve: Serve the jollof beans hot, garnished with the fried plantain.

Tips

For an extra creamy texture, you can mash a portion of the cooked beans before adding them to the stew. This helps to thicken the dish naturally. Using palm oil is traditional and gives the dish its authentic flavor, but you can substitute it with vegetable oil if necessary.
